From SAR Scene to Crop Map: Automated Agricultural Classification, Atbasar Region

The problem

Mapping crop distribution across large agricultural areas is time-intensive when done manually, and optical imagery is unreliable in cloudy or rain-heavy seasons. The task required a scalable, weather-independent approach to identifying crop types across the Atbasar region for Summer 2021.

The approach

  • Built an end-to-end pipeline using three Sentinel-1 SLC scenes. Preprocessing in ESA SNAP covered debursting, radiometric calibration, terrain correction, covariance matrix generation, and model-based polarimetric decomposition (surface, volume, double-bounce). Output: a multi-channel GeoTIFF combining SAR backscatter and decomposition layers across all acquisition dates.
  • Extracted pixel-level training data from field polygons and prepared labelled samples for supervised learning.
  • Trained a Random Forest classifier in Python using chunk-based processing to handle large multi-date rasters without memory constraints.
  • Applied post-classification filtering to remove noise and isolated misclassified regions.

The result

The model reliably identified wheat distribution across the study area. Barley mapping was excluded from the final output due to insufficient training samples — a deliberate decision that preserved map accuracy over false precision. The pipeline demonstrated strong potential for multi-season SAR crop monitoring with expanded reference data.

Stack: ESA SNAP · Python (scikit-learn, rasterio, NumPy) · Sentinel-1 SLC

Quantifying Two Decades of Urban Greening: Astana NDVI Time-Series (2002–2024)

The problem

Urban greening investments in Astana are difficult to evaluate without longitudinal spatial data. Decision-makers needed evidence of whether vegetation cover had actually improved across the city's districts over time — and where targeted interventions were still needed.

The approach

I analyzed satellite-derived NDVI data across four time points (2002, 2015, 2019, 2024) at the district level, mapping vegetation density and change patterns across Esil, Almaty, Nura, Saryarka, and Baikonyr.

The result

  • Esil District showed the strongest gains, driven by new parks and planted forest belts.
  • Almaty District demonstrated consistent improvement across all four time points.
  • Nura and Saryarka showed gradual vegetation recovery in peripheral zones.
  • Baikonyr, dominated by industrial land use, remained the lowest-performing district — a clear candidate for targeted greening policy.

NDVI values across the city ranged from −0.1 to 0.5, establishing a quantitative baseline for ongoing monitoring. The analysis confirmed a consistent citywide greening trend over 22 years and demonstrated how remote sensing can replace assumption-based urban planning with measurable evidence.

Stack: ArcGIS / QGIS · Sentinel-2 / Landsat · NDVI time-series analysis · Thematic cartography
